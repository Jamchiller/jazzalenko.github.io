Marketing Strategy

I’ve worked with both start-ups and established companies in a range of industries to develop their marketing strategies. By focussing on the big picture and then tailoring solutions that specifically fit the budget, scope and objectives of the business, I’ve been able to give these companies a strong foundation from which they can grow.

More recently, I’ve spent two and a half years managing the marketing of one of the largest POS and marketing services companies in Europe, with over 600 employees and £70m turnover. I manage the various aspects of the promotional mix, including advertisements, lead generation, PR, digital marketing, awards and industry relations, case studies, and brand management. This strategy is lead by a marketing and sales plan produced in collaboration with the Sales Director and approved by the board.

During my time at The Delta Group, the company has experienced a period of sustained revenue growth, and the marketing function has seamlessly adapted to complement this growth. I’ve overseen a complete restructure of the department through this period, including an expansion of its size, budget & scope. A more balanced mix of integrated marketing communications has been introduced, with new marketing channels opened - digitally, through LinkedIn and Twitter, and through traditional channels such as trade press advertisements and email marketing.

Brand equity (especially brand awareness) significantly increased during this period. We were shortlisted for and won a number of multi-million pound tenders and won one of the largest industry accolades (Display of the Year at the POPAI Awards 2014). On the annual industry ‘Power’ rankings (the Printweek Power 100), our CEO moved up from 32nd to 16th within two years - a strong indicator of the company’s improved reputation.


Working at a leading marketing services firm specialising in print, I also have a detailed knowledge of POS campaigns at every stage, from creative to activation.

New Product Launches

Screenfast: A Case Study.

Objective: Manage the successful, multi-channel launch of a new technology service into the film industry, aiming to revolutionise the distribution of films into cinemas through the use of cloud services and proprietary software installed onto the cinemas’ computers.

Challenge: Screenfast was a small fry in comparison to some of its larger competitors, who all had different solutions to the ‘digital delivery’ question. We had a relatively limited marketing budget and resources. The main question would be whether to focus on convincing cinemas to trial the system, and hope for a bottom-up conversion, or to try to directly convince distributors to use the system over our competitors’.

Above: Early-stage branding.

Execution: We identified our key competitive advantage - the fact that the system used the internet (a ‘younger’ and more future-proof solution). We built our branding around this - young, fun, agile, and tech-savvy. A multi-platform marketing campaign was developed, targeting both independent and franchise cinema chains. Some of the key activities including running advertisements in trade press that made use of white space to cut through noise; building a website that we could drive traffic towards; partnering with major trade press to promote our brand organically; and carrying out live demonstrations at European trade shows.

Above: Article in target trade press & magazine advertisement.

Results: Currently, more than 70% of independent cinemas use Screenfast on a regular basis to receive their DCPs. We’re now in talks with a number of major distributors thanks to the success with independents.

  • Service: New Product Development
